SMOKE MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS

Staircase Pressurization Fan

Designed to keep stairwells free from smoke during a fire, these fans create positive pressure to ensure safe evacuation.

Staircase Lobby Pressurization Fan

Similar to staircase pressurization, these fans maintain positive pressure in lobbies, preventing smoke infiltration.

Lift Shaft Pressurization Fan

These fans ensure that smoke does not enter the lift shaft, protecting occupants using elevators during emergencies.

Smoke Exhaust Fan

Smoke exhaust fans help remove smoke and hot gases from buildings during a fire, enhancing visibility and reducing temperature.

Jet Fans

Jet fans are typically used in large spaces like parking garages to manage smoke and air quality during both normal operation and emergencies.

Supply Air Fan

Supply air fans circulate fresh air into the building, maintaining indoor air quality and helping in pressurization systems.

CO System for Basement


A carbon monoxide monitoring system ensures that CO levels in basements are kept within safe limits by controlling ventilation fans.

Pre-QCDD & QCDD Inspections:

Essential Tests We Provide We perform the following tests to ensure compliance with QCDD standards:

Testing, Adjusting, and Balancing (TAB)

Ensuring your system is operating at optimal efficiency by adjusting airflow and pressure.

Smoke Test (Pre-QCDD & QCDD Inspections)

Verifying the proper operation of smoke control systems, ensuring smoke is safely exhausted.

Differential Pressure Test

Conducted for staircases, lobbies, and lift shafts to confirm proper pressurization.

Air Velocity Test for Staircase

Ensuring that air velocity in stairwells meets safety standards for proper evacuation.

Door Force Test

Checking the force required to open doors under pressurized conditions, ensuring easy egress during emergencies.
